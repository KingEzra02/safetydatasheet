OSHA Electrical Safety Standard 1926.431 - SDS Web Page

General Maintenance and Upkeep:

  • Employers and employees must monitor equipment and machinery to watch out for loose screws, gaskets, and other pieces that may interfere with dust and explosion prevention.
  • Scheduled maintenance should follow manufacturer guidelines to ensure safe operation.
  • Damaged or worn components must be replaced immediately to maintain safety standards.

Reporting and Repair:

  • If a worker spots any issues with machinery, they must report to a supervisor immediately.
  • Once a report is filed, the employer must stop using the circuit until the problem is fixed.
  • Repairs should only be performed by qualified personnel, and records must be kept for compliance.

Dust-Proofing:

  • Dust can get into machinery and cause various problems, both mechanical and electrical.
  • Employers must protect all gear from dust-related incidents that could lead to an explosion or electrical arc.
  • Sealed housings, proper ventilation, and routine cleaning are required to minimize dust accumulation.

Hazardous Locations

  • The standard specifically addresses equipment used in areas where flammable gases, vapors, or combustible dusts may be present.
  • Specialized electrical equipment must be certified for use in hazardous (classified) locations.
  • Proper zoning and classification of hazardous areas are required to ensure appropriate safety measures.

Explosion-Proofing

  • Employers must maintain equipment so that there is little to no risk of an explosion due to electrical malfunction.
  • Explosion-proof enclosures must be used in hazardous locations to contain sparks or heat.
  • Regular inspections should ensure seals, wiring, and components remain intact to prevent ignition sources
